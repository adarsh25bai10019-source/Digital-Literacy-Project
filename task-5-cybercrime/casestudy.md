# 💳 Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime where attackers trick users into transferring money or revealing sensitive banking details. A typical scenario involves a student receiving a call or message from someone pretending to be a bank official or an online buyer/seller.

## 🔍 How the Fraud Happens
1. The attacker contacts the victim and creates urgency or trust (e.g., “Your account will be blocked”).
2. They send a fake UPI collect request or phishing link.
3. The victim is asked to approve the request or enter their UPI PIN.
4. Entering the PIN actually authorizes a payment from the victim’s account.
5. Sometimes, attackers also ask for OTPs or use screen-sharing apps to gain access.
   
## 🎯 Who is Targeted
- Students
- Elderly people
- Users unfamiliar with digital payments

## ⚠️ Consequences
- Financial loss
- Data theft
- Emotional stress and panic
- Difficulty in recovering money
  
## 🚀 Key Takeaway
Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Always verify before approving any request.
